# About This Project

## Research Focus

This PhD research explores bacterial genomics and antimicrobial resistance in foodborne pathogens, specifically examining *Campylobacter* and *Salmonella* in New Zealand through a One Health perspective.

## Main Research Questions

1. What is the diversity and mobility potential of plasmids across *Campylobacter* isolates in New Zealand?
2. How have *Salmonella* Bovismorbificans and Give serovars emerged and circulated through New Zealand populations?
3. What are the patterns of antimicrobial resistance and horizontal gene transfer in these pathogens?
4. How do genomic findings inform surveillance and public health strategies?

## Methodologies

- **High-performance computing**: NeSI infrastructure for large-scale bioinformatics analysis
- **Genomic analysis**: Whole-genome sequencing, long-read technologies, assembly and annotation
- **Phylogenetic methods**: MLST, cgMLST, phylogenetic reconstruction with GrapeTree and R
- **Comparative genomics**: Plasmid characterization, synteny analysis, resistance profiling
- **Bioinformatics pipelines**: chewBACCA, custom R/Python/Bash workflows

## Tools and Languages

- **R**: Phylogenetic visualization (ggtree, aplot), comparative genomics (gggenomes), data analysis and plotting
- **Bash**: Workflow automation, HPC job scheduling, sequence processing
- **Python**: Data processing, custom analysis scripts
- **Bioinformatics tools**: PubMLST, GrapeTree, chewBACCA, BLAST, alignment tools
