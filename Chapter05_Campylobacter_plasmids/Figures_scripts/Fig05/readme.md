#### Description

Figure 5 - Dendrogram based on the presence/absence of genes amongst the plasmid contigs from this study and the reference plasmid from C. jejuni strain 15AR0917 (CP035896.1). The dendrogram was generated based on the gene presence/absence binary matrix data from Roary  and visualised with ggtree. The dots on the tree tips display the Campylobacter species to which the isolates belong. The shapes of the filled symbols represent the mobility prediction of each plasmid.  The labels display the ID and the sequence type (ST) of the isolate harbouring the plasmid contig. The colour strip adjacent to the dendrogram shows the source of isolation of the bacterial isolates. The black and white strips show if the respective plasmid sequences were circularisable or not according to Bandage. The heatmap displays the presence and absence of the plasmid marker combinations within each plasmid sequence. Red squares indicate the presence and grey absence of the respective markers’ combination. “NP” stands for “Not present” which indicates there were no known plasmid markers in that contig. The bar plot shows the length of each plasmid sequence. The dumbbell plot compares the GC content of the plasmid sequences with their corresponding host chromosome, with the purple line showing the plasmid GC content average percentage and the two green lines showing the GC content average percentage of C. coli and C. jejuni chromosomes.
